Explore The Garden of Ireland

Glendalough Upper Lake

Nestled in the heart of the Wicklow Mountains, Glendalough is a haven of tranquility. Famous for its monastic ruins and scenic walks, it's a must-visit for history buffs and nature lovers alike.

Powerscourt Waterfall

Ireland's tallest waterfall, Powerscourt cascades down 121 meters, set against a backdrop of lush woodlands. A picture-perfect spot for a family picnic or a serene nature escape.

Wicklow Head Cliff Walk

Experience dramatic coastal scenery and panoramic ocean views on this stunning cliff walk. It's a favorite among hikers and photographers for its breathtaking views and diverse wildlife.

Wicklow Head Lighthouse

Standing tall since the early 19th century, this iconic lighthouse offers a glimpse into maritime history. Part of the Irish Landmark Trust Experience, the lighthouse is available to rent for a truly once-in-a-lifetime stay.

Beyond The Trees Avondale

Explore this award-winning tree canopy walkway, offering an immersive journey through Avondale's ancient woodlands. Perfect for families and adventure seekers.

The Murrough Wicklow

Stretching along the coast, The Murrough is a diverse wetland area perfect for birdwatching, fishing and leisurely walks. Its serene landscapes make it a peaceful getaway from urban life.

National Botanical Gardens Kilmacurragh

Known for its rare and exotic plants, Kilmacurragh offers a botanical wonderland. Wander through beautifully landscaped gardens and discover unique horticultural treasures.

Lough Tay AKA 'Guinness Lake'

Dubbed "Guinness Lake" for its dark, peaty waters, Lough Tay is a postcard-perfect glacial lake. It's a gem for photographers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Greenan Maze

Get lost in the fun and challenge of Greenan Maze, set amidst the picturesque Wicklow countryside. A wonderful day out for families and puzzle lovers.
